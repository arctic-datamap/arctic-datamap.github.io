By dividing the world’s snow covers into different types, we can match the appropriate tools to each snow type and confounding factor. The various snow classes take into account the wind, precipitation, and temperature regimes these snow covers evolve within, and that depth, density, number of layers, grain characteristics, metamorphic trajectories and melt sequences differ across these various types of snow.
